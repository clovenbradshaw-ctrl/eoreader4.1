I.
The house at Esker stood above the river, its grey stone darkened by a century of rain.
Old Mathilde had kept the house for forty years, and her two children had grown beneath its steep roofs.
Felix was the elder, restless and proud, and Liesl was his sister, gentle and watchful.
Liesl loved Felix dearly, and as children the two of them were inseparable in the orchard below the house.
Tomas was the neighbour's son, a quiet boy who worked the land that bordered Esker.
In the early years Felix and Tomas fished the river together as friends.
II.
Mathilde died in the spring, and the house fell silent for a season.
The will left Esker to Felix, and to Liesl only their mother's garden and a small annuity.
Felix had expected the whole estate, and the division gnawed at him through the long winter.
He spoke little at supper, and his silences grew heavier as the months passed.
The river rose and fell with the rains, and the orchard greened and faded again.
Felix began to blame Tomas for the falling yields, though Tomas had only ever helped the family.
He taunted Tomas at the market, calling him a hired hand who reached above his station.
Liesl begged Felix to let the matter rest, but he would not hear her.
The grandfather's gold watch, the one treasure the will had not named, went missing from the hall.
The household whispered that Felix had taken the watch, though no one had seen him do so.
Felix denied it coldly and turned the blame again upon Tomas.
He mocked Tomas before the servants, and twice he drove the boy from the door.
Liesl watched her brother harden into a man she no longer recognised.
At the harvest supper Felix accused Tomas of the theft before all the guests.
Felix turned upon Liesl and named her a traitor for defending the hired boy.
Liesl rose from the table and renounced Felix before the whole house.
She swore she would never again call Felix her brother, and she left Esker that same night.
Felix sat alone in the cold hall, the empty watch-stand before him.
Tomas left the valley before the first snow and never came back.
III.
Winter closed over Esker, and the grey stone darkened once more with rain.
The orchard stood bare, and the river ran black beneath the ice.
Liesl took a room in the town and let the long years pass without a word.
Felix kept the house, but not the family that had once filled it.
Felix and Liesl never reconciled, and the watch was never found.
